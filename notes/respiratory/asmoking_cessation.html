nicotine replacement therapy

  • effectively aids smoking cessaiotn

    • doubling chances of long term abstinence

    • NNT = 14

      • @ 6mo

  • explain what results may be achieved

    • reduce cravings

    • reduce relapse

    • doubles chances of quiting

    • reduces weight gain

  • reassure that nrt is safe

    • nrt safer than smoking in pregnancy

    • if had MI in last 2wks discuss with specialist

  • use enough

    • moderate advantage to using combination over single

    • if within \<30min between waking and cig then use 2x patches (42mg)

  • use NRT for long enough time

    • 8-12wks

    • may need to use for longer

  • use NRT in way that suits

dose

  • after 1hr of waking

    • \<10/day

      • 2mg gum or 1mg lozenge or 14mg patch

    • >10/day

      • 21mg patchy with either 2mg gum or 1mg lozenge

  • within 1hr of waking

    • \<10/day

      • 21mg patch with 2mg gum/1mg lozenge

    • >19/day

      • 21mg patch with 4mg gum / 2mg lozenge

  • patches

    • most smokers start on full-strength path

    • lower doses used for weaning off

    • new site each day

  • Gum

    • gum slwoly chewed

      • hot peppery taste

    • parked between cheek and gums so can be absorbed

    • gum chewed again after few minutes and parked

    • repeated for 20-30mins

    • 20min to reach peak concentration

    • 10-15pieces of gum/day

      • hourly if needed

    • use is recommended for 8wks

  • loznege

    • slowly sucked and dissolved in mouth

    • peak concentration 20-30mins

Nortriptyline

  • almost doubles chances of long-term abstinence

  • as effective as NRT or bupropion

  • NNT = 11

    • independent of antidepressant effect

  • common side effects

    • drowsinesss

    • dry mouth

    • constipation

    • nausea

  • dangerous in overdose

  • adverse effects on cardiovascular funtion

    • ECG changes

    • arrhythmia
