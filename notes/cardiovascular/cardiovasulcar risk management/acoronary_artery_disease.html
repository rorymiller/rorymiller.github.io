coronary arter disease
  • Management
  • medications

chest pain due to transient myocardial ischamia

relieved by rest or sublingula nitroglycerin

Management

  • most important intervention = smoking cesastion

  • weight loss not shown to increase survival

medications

  • aspirin

    • NNT 42 - ISIS2

    • given routinely and continued for life

  • ticagrelor

  • clopidogrel

    • effective alternative

    • often in combination = 1yr

    • 75mg

  • Warfarin

    • AF/PAF

    • large left ventricular aneurysm

    • thrombus in LV at infarction site on ECHO

    • systemic embolism

    • INR 2.5 (2-3)

  • Betablockers

    • NNT 143; ISIS1

    • considered for everyone unless CI

    • also with LV dysfunction

    • initial dose low

    • give at night

      • reduce risks of postural hypotension

      • alleviate symptoms of tiredness and lethargy

    • before discontinuing beta-blockers because of side-effects, lower dose or alternative betablocker should be tried

  • ACEi

    • NNT 22 - SOLVD

    • should be considred

    • especially:

      • ant. infarct/

      • LV dysfucntion

      • CHF

    • PROGRESS/HOPE

      • favourable outcomes independent of a blood pressure effect post MI

  • Statins

    • NNT 33 - CARE

    • should be started after MI

  • CCB

    • rate limiting non-dihydropyridine CCB

      • verapamil

      • diltiazem

  • Nitrates

    • symptom relief

    • not indicated for reducing risk of further events
