polyarticular arthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis:
  • References

  • 1yr after onset of undifferentiated inflammatory arthrtiis

    • 21% RA

    • 36 undifferentiated

    • 24% reactive arthritis

    • 7% psoriatic arthritis

  • consistent independent predictors of unfavourable outcome in recent onset, undifferentiated arthritis

    • polyarticular onset

    • positive anti CCP

    • positive RA

    • joint erosion on XR at presentation

    • disease duration greater than 3-6mo

  • CRP elevation highly variable in inflammatory arthritis

    • can be absent in about 40% with recent onset disease

      • wouldn’t exlude diagnosis of RA

  • if ESR >100 or CRP >120 consider other diagnoses

    • infection

    • vasculitis

    • crystal arthritis

  • rheumatoid factor or anti-CCP

    • strongly positive RF inc. likelihood of early RA

      • particularly in pts. with polyarticular onset of disease

      • correlates with less favourable outcome

    • RF:

      • absent 38% with RA

      • falsey elevated

        • lupus

        • Sjogren’s syndrome

        • acute/chronic infection

        • cryoglobulinaemia

        • lymphoproliferative disorder

      • also in 1-2% of healthy individuals

        • 20% age >65

    • AntiCCP

      • greater specificity

      • particularly useful neg. for RF where RA suspected

      • strongest independent predictor of radiographic progression

Rheumatoid arthritis:

  • 29/100 000 F and 18/100 000 M

  • cause unknown

    • genetic and environmental factors considered improtant

  • gradual onset

  • formal diagnositc criteria perform very poorly in early disease

    • 2010 American college or rheumatology/european league against rheumatism

    • at least 1 joint with definite clinical synovitis and not better explained by another

    • score of =>6/10

    • joint involvement

    • one large = 0

    • 2-10 large = 1

    • 1-3 small (+/- large) = 2

    • 4-10 small 3

    • >10 (at least 1 small) 5

    • Serology

    • negatvie RF and neg CCP = 0

    • low posirtive RF or CCP = 2

    • high positive = 3 (either)

    • acute phase reactants

    • N CRP/ESR = 0

    • inc. CRP/ESR = 1

    • duration

    • \<6wk = 0

    • => 6wk = 1

  • risk factors;

    • older age

    • family history of diseases

    • female sex

    • current/past cigarette

  • Typical presentation

    • pain and stiffness in multiple joints

    • PIPJ/MCPJ most commonly involved

    • morning stiffness >1hr = inflammatory arthritis

    • boggy swellin = synovitis

    • systematic symptoms may occur with active disease:

      • fatigue

      • weight loss

      • low-grade fever

  • extra-articular manisfestations

    • 2x inc. risk of lymphoma

      • underlyign inflammatory process

      • not b/c medical treatment

    • inc. risk of:

      • coronary artery disease
