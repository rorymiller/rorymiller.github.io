  • RA chornic autoimmune disease

    • inflammation of synovial tissue in joints

      • swelling

      • pain

      • stiffness

      • joint destruction

    • spont. remission \<5%

  • aim of treatment = minimal joint inflammation

    • therapeutic remission

  • start treatmetn ASAP

    • early treatment = improve outcomes

  • degree of inflamation = closely related to joint damage

  • synovitis >6wk to rheumatologist

Methotrexate

  • first line

  • moderate - severe RA

    • effective

    • predictable adverse effect profile

    • simple to administer

    • inexpensive

  • highly active idsease -> combination

  • mild disease

    • sulfasalzine or hydroxychloroquine

  • response often 1-2mo

Monitoring:

  • CBC

    • b/l then 2wks until stable 6wks then monthly

    • look for pancytopenia

      • discuss with specialist team immediately

    • MCV >105

      • check b12, folate , tsh and treat

  • LFT

    • b/l; 2wks until stable then montly

    • look for AST, ALT twice upper limit

      • withold and discuss

      • other factors:

        • etoh

        • NSAID

        • reveiw medciations

  • Cr

    • b/l; 2 weeks until stable 6wk then monthly

    • reduce dose if deterioration

  • rash or oral ulceration

    • w/h until discuss

    • folicnic acid mouth wash may help with mucositis

  • nausea and vomiting, diarrhoea

    • give s/c

  • new dyspnoea and cough

    • withold and idcuss urgently

    • arange cxr and resp function tests

  • severe sore throat, abnormal bleeidng

    • immediate CBC and w/h

prescribing points

  • once weekly oral or by injection

  • coprescribe with 5mg folic acid

    • avoid same day

  • interactions

    • accumulates renal impairment

    • TMP and cotrimoxazole interact with MTX and significantly increase risk of marrow aplasia

  • ETOh

    • 1/2 level normal population

  • Flu vaccination

    • annual influenza

  • ontraception

    • known teratogen

    • effective contraceptoin

    • cont. for 3mo after stopping

Sulfasalazine

  • often next step

  • with metotrexate, suflasalzine and hydroxychloroquine

Monitoring:

  • CBC

    • b/l, 2 wks for first 2mo then monthly 3mo then 3mo

      • pancytopenia

      • macrocytosis

        • see MTX

    • LFT

      • baseline then 2wk for first 2mo then monthly 3mo then 3mo

        • ALT, AST twice ULN

    • Nausea/dizziness/headahces

      • if poss continue

      • may have to reduce dose or stop if symptoms severe

      • discuss

    • AbN brusing or sore thorat

      • check CBC

    • unexplained acute widespread rash

      • withhold and discuss immediately

    • oral ulceration

      • withold and discuss

prescribing

  • once daily

  • interaction

    • reduces absoption of digoxin

    • combination need not avoided

    • observed for signs of under digitilisation

  • can be used in pregnancy

    • does not >2g/day

    • folic acid

    • causes reversible oligospermia

  • yellow discoloration

    • urine

    • tears

Hydroxychloroquine

  • response 5mo

  • may increase skin’s sensitivity

  • blueblack discoloration of skin may occur with long-term use

  • photosensitivty

Monitoring:

  • visual disturbance

    • baseline opthalmological review

      • low risk q5yr

        • \<60yo

        • no liver disease

        • no retinal disease

      • high risk annual

    • discuss with opthalmologist immediately

lefluonomide

  • available special authority

    • not adequately responded to MTX and sulfasalazine

  • can be used alone or in combination wiht MTX

  • often less tolerated

Monitoring:

  • cbc

    • baseline then q3wk for first 5mo

    • if stable 2mo

    • 4wkly if co-prescribed

    • pancyctopenia

  • LFT

    • b;l; q4/52 6/12, 8/52. if coprescribe mo

    • AST,ALT,ALP 2x ULN

  • BP

    • b/l

    • each visit

    • if >140/90

      • treat

      • if remains uncontrolled - discuss

  • abnormal bruising or severe sore thraot

    • CBC

  • new or increase dyspnoea or dry cough

    • CXR

    • resp function

Cyclosporin and azathioprine

  • usually reserved for patients who are unresponsive to other DMARDs

    • increase risk of adverse effects

  • cyclosporin = nephrotoxicity
