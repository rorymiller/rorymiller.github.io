  • most common metabolic bone disease after osteoporosis

  • 2-4% adults >55yo

  • aetiology only partly understood

  • both genetic and environmental factors

  • maybe asymptomatic

    • incidental: radiology, biochemistry

  • may present with bone pain, deformity, fracture

  • bisphosphonate therapy

    • symptomatic disease

      • also risk of complications

        • long bones

        • vertebrae

        • base of skull

  • mainly effects elderly

  • most commonly affects axial skelton

    • pelvis

    • lumbosacral spine

    • skull

    • femur or tibia

  • may be localised or widespread

investigations

  • LFT

  • radiography of affected bone

    • lytic actibity predominates

    • focla osetolytic lesions

    • or flame shaped, advancing lytic wedges

  • subsequent areas of sclerosis develop

    • mixed lytic and sclerotic areas

    • thickened trabeculae

    • bone explnsion

    • corticla thickening

    • deformity

  • isotop bone scan more senstiive than radiography

biochemisty

  • ALP most clinically useful indicator of disease actiity

  • elevated in most untreated patients

Treatment

  • mainstay = bisphosphonate

    • reduce bone turonover

    • improve bone apin

    • promote healing of osteolytic lesions

    • improve QOL

  • indications:

    • pain in pagetic bones

    • neurological complications

    • significant osteolytic lesions

    • involvement of long bones, vertebrae or base of skull

    • before surgery involving pagetic bones

    • significant joint involvement

  • zolendronic acid 5mg single dose

  • Alendromnate 40mg daily orally for 3-6mo

  • Risedronate 30mg daily given for 2mo

  • response to treatment monitered by measuring ALP until noramilised then annually

  • relapse is common
