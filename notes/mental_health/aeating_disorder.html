Anorexia nervosa

pathological fear of weight gain associated with overwhelming drive for thinness

  • other presenting features;

    • altered eating or dietary behaviour

    • excesive exercise

    • amenorrhoea

    • depressed mood and/or withdrawal from scoial contact

  • single GP consultaiton for eating behaviour or weight and shape concernes

    • significant predictor for subsequent emergence of eating disorder

  • other symptoms of under nutrition:

    • dizziness

    • fatigue

    • headache

    • abdominal symptoms

      • nausea

      • bloating

      • unexplained vomiting

      • lack of appeitie

      • constipation

DSM V

  • persistent restriction of energy intake leaading to significantly low body weight

    • context of what is minimaly expected for age, sex, developmental trajectory and physical health

  • either an intense fear of gaining weight or becoming fat or persistent behaviour that interferes with weight gain

  • disturbance in way one’s body weight or shape is experienced, undue influence of body shape and weight on self-evaluation, or peristent lack of recognition of seriousness of current low body weight

  • (amenorrheoa = absence of at least 3 consecutive)

Clinical questions:

  • how do you feel about your weight?

  • How much do you think you should weigh?

  • do you or anyone else have any concernes about your eating or exercise behaviours
