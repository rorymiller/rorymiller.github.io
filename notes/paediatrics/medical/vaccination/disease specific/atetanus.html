Tetanus

Tetanus
  • Wound control
  • Clinical

Mode: environmental exposure to bacillus

incubation: 3-21d; commonly 10d

Vaccine: Toxoid containing

  • DTaP-IPV-HepB/Hib

  • DTap-IPV

  • Tdap

  • Td

  • 6wk, 3mo, 5mo, 4yrs, 11yrs, 45,65, pregnancy

effectiveness

too rare to test effectiveness

  • tetanus toxin

    • Clostridium tetani

      • Gram + bacillus (anaerobe)

Wound control

  • following injury

    • clean and devitalised tissue removed

  • if tetanus prone and doubt re immunisation

    • TIG

    • \3 dose primary immunisation course

  • tetanus prone =

    • compound #

    • bite wounds

    • deep, penetrating wounds

    • wounds containing foreign bodies

    • complicated by pyogenic infections

    • extensive tissue damage

    • superficial wound obviously contaminated soil, dust, horse manure

      • especially if delayed 4hrs

    • reimplantation of avulsed tooth

  • Guide

    • ≥ 3 doses

      • \<5 years

        • No Td/Tdap or TIG

      • 5-10 yrs

        • clean minor

          • No Td/Tdap or TIG

        • tetanus prone

          • booster dose

      • >10yrs (all tetanus prone)

        • booster

    • \< 3 doses

      • clean minor

        • complete course immunisations

      • tetanus prone wounds

        • complete course

        • TIG

          • 250IU IM

            • ASAP

            • if >24hours

              • 500IU

Clinical

  • muscular rigidity

    • painful contraction spasms

      • characteristic facial grimace

      • arching back

        • (opisthotonus)

  • remains alert until severely hypoxic

  • initial symptoms

    • weakness, stiffnes, cramps

    • difficult chewing

  • 10 overall mortality

    • higher in older people
