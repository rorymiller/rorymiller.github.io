Poliomyelitis

Poliomyelitis
  • clinical

Mode of transmisison: faecal-oral route

Incubation: paralytic disease - 7-14d

Period of infectivity: most infectious in days immediately before and after onset of symptoms

Vaccines:  inactivated

  • DTaP-IPV-HepB/Hib

  • DTaP-IPV

  • IPV

Schedule: 6wk, 3mo, 5mo, 4

efficacy: >90%

Herd:

  • highly transmissible

    • small, non enveloped enterovirus

clinical

  • fever

  • headache

  • gastrointestinal disturbances

  • malaise

  • stiffness of neck and back

  • pain in the limbs, back and neck

  • with or without paralysis
