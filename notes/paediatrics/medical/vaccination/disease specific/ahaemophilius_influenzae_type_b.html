Haemophilus influenzae type b - Hib disease

Haemophilus influenzae type b - Hib disease
  • Clinical

Mode of transmisison: droplet - inhalation

Incubation: unkown 2-4d

Period of infectivity: may be rpolonged; non-communicable within 24-48hrs after starting

Vaccines: conjugated

  • DTaP-IPV-HepB/Hib

  • Hib-PRP-T

  • no specific contraindications

Schedule: 6wk, 3mo, 5mo, 15mo

efficacy: virtually elimination Hib disease

Herd:

  • gram negative cocoobacillus

    • typeable and non-typeable

    • type b = most important

Clinical

  • causes meningitis, other focal infections

    • pneumonia

    • septic arthritis

    • cellulitis

    • \<2yo

    • EPIGLOTTITIS

  • rare >5yo

  • infected individuals with invasive Hib disease \<2yo don’t reliably produce protective antibodies

  • Rifampicin chemoprophylaxis should be given to contacts ASAP

    • prophylaxis started after 7d may still be of some benefit
