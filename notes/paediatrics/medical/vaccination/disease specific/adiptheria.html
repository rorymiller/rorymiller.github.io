Diptheria

Diptheria
  • Clinical

Mode of transmisison: droplet/infected skin contact

Incubation: 2-5d

Period of infectivity: 2wks or less

Vaccines: cell-free purified diptheria toxoid

  • DTaP-IPV-HepB/Hib (infanrix-hexa)

  • DTaP-IPV (infanrix-IPV)

  • Tdap (boostrix)

  • Td (ADT Booster)

  • no specific contraindications

Schedule: 6wks,3mo,5mo,4yo, 11yo, 45,65, pregnancy

efficacy: 87-98% protection; immunised = less severe

Herd: ≥ 70%must be immune to prevent major community outbreaks

  • serious, often fatal, toxin mediated disease

  • Corynebacterium diphtheriae

    • gram positive

Clinical

  • membranous inflammation of upper respirator tract

  • involvement of other tissues

    • myocardium

    • periperhal nerves

  • humans = only known host

  • gradual onset 1-2d

    • mildly painful tonsillitis with associated greyish membrane

      • suspected if membrane involves ulvula and soft palate

  • complication = respiratory obstruction
