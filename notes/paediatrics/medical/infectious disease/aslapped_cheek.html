erythema infectiosum

erythema infectiosum
  • Clinical

aka slapped cheek

fifth disease

  • parvovirus b10

  • causes fetal anaemia and hydrops fetalis after primary infection

  • commmon

Clinical

  • incubation 3-14d

  • rarely last 21d

  • benign

    • mild nonspecific illness

  • rash

    • 3 stages

      • slapped cheek rash

        • cirumoral pallor

      • erythematous symmetrical maculopapular trunkal rash

        • 1-4d later

      • lacy, reticulated rash

        • 2-40d

        • may be pruritic

        • doens’t desquamate

  • Older people

    • myalgia

    • significant arthlagia

    • arthritis

    • children with shortened erythrocyte life span

      • transient aplastic crisis
