Measles
  • Microbiology
  • Clinical
  • complications
  • exclude

Microbiology

  • spread

    • coughing and sneezing

    • also direct contact with the nose/throat secretions of an infected person

  • incubation

    • 7-19d

    • (10d onset

    • (14d rash

  • infectious

    • first day of illness until 4d after rash begins

Clinical

  • prodromal phase of 2-4days with:

    • fever

    • 3 C’s

      • cough

      • conjunctivitis

      • coryza

    • Koplik’s spots

  • maculopapular rash

    • images

    • first behind ears on 3-7d

    • speads over from head and face -> trunk

  • convalescnet stage

    • temporary brownish stain on skin

  • vitamin a associated with dec mortality/morbidity

    • OD for 2d at:

      • 200 000IU ≥12mo

      • 100 000 IU 6-11mo

      • 50 000 IU \<6mo

  • modified measles

    • mild cases of measles occuring in persons with partial protection against measles

      • occurs in persons vaccinated \<12mo

complications

  • AOM

    • most common

  • pneumonia

  • croup

  • diarrhoea

  • encephalitis:

    • 1:1000

      • 15% die

      • 25-35% left with permanent neurological damage

  • sub-acute sclerosing panencephalitis

    • fatal

    • typcially 7-10yrs after wild type measles virus infection

exclude

  • 4d from onset of rash
