Community acquired pneumonia
  • Signs and symptoms

  • follow-up

  • common cause of hosptial admission in adults

  • 3x more prevalent maori ccompared to non maori

    • maori higher mortality 6x

  • risk factors:

    • low SES

    • poor nutrition

    • low birth weight

    • reduced rates of breast feeding

    • exposure to tobacco smoker

    • lower housing quality

      • insulation

      • heating

      • damp

      • moulud

      • overcrowded conditions

      • reduced access to primary healthcare

  • Hospital vs community

    • hospital in >2d in previous 90d

      • hospital acquired pneumonia

      • different pathogenesis

Signs and symptoms

  • fever

  • tachypnoea

    • \<2mo

      • >60 (severe = >70)

    • 2-12mo

      • ≥50 (severe >60)

    • >12mo

      • >40 (severe >50)

  • increase respiratory efffort

  • irritability, fatigue

  • difficulty feeding

  • dyspnoea, stridor or wheeze

  • cough (less common in infants)

  • pleuritic chest pain

signs less frequently found in young children

  • hgih fever, tachycardia, increase resp effort/rate may be only signs

  • consider inhaled FB

investigations

  • not usually required

  • CXR may be considered where clinical diagnosis difficult or unclear

    • history suggestive of FB aspiration

    • chest signs

      • suggest pleural effusion/collapse

Referral

  • \<6mo

  • drinking less than half noraml amount

  • O2 sat ≤92%

  • severe tachypnoea/apnoea

  • increase respiratory effort

  • signs of fatigue

  • temp \<35 or >40

  • decreased breath sounds or dullness to percussion

  • where there is a concern that home-based care will not provide careful observation, compliance with treatment recommendations or follw-up if symptoms worsen

management

  • usually caused by strep pneumoniae

    • viruses

    • chlamydia pneumonia

    • mycoplasma pneumoniae

    • haemophilus influenza

    • staphylococcus aureus

  • always abx; even if viral infection suspected

    • emperic - not targeted towards specific organism

  • laboratory investigation not routinely recommended

    • isolates pathogen in 2-50% of infections only

  • first line:

    • Amoxicillin 25mg/kg tds for 7d

  • atypcial infections uncommon \<5yo

    • if >5yo and treatment fails then consider

    • Erythromycin 10mg/kg four times daily for 7d

  • hydration = improtant

    • frequent amounts more frequently

follow-up

  • most children show improvmenet within 24-48hrs of antibiotic treatment

  • continue to improve over time

  • persistent symptoms

    • review as cough adn mild shortness of breath on exertion may persisit for several weeks

  • if atelectasis on cxr

    • repeat in 6wks and refer if collapse hasn’t resolved
