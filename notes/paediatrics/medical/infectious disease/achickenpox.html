Varicella (chicken pox)

Varicella (chicken pox)
  • incubation
  • early signs
  • infectious period
  • complications
  • exclusion

  • how spread:

    • coughing and sneezing

    • also direct contact with weeping blisters

incubation

  • 14-16d (10-21)

early signs

  • prodromal symptoms - may precede by 24hrs

    • fever

    • malaise

    • anorexia

  • characteristic rash

    • small red papules

    • rapidly progress -> nonumbilicated, oval, tear-drop vesicels on erytheamtous base

    • fluid progresses:

      • clear - cloudy

    • vesicles ulcerate, crush and heal

    • new crops appear 3-4d

    • trunk -> head -> face

    • pruritis = universal

    • lesions may be presnet on mucous membranes

infectious period

  • from up to 5d before appearance of rash until lesions have crusted (usually about 5d)

complications

  • secondary infection of skin lesions by strep/staph

    • most common

  • Pneumonia is uncommon in healthy children

    • may complicate up to 15-20% of healthy adults and immunocompromised persons

  • uncommon:

    • myocarditis

    • pericardiits

    • orchitis

    • hepatitis

    • ulcerative gastritis

    • acute GN

    • arthritis

  • Reye sydrome may follow

  • Neurological complications

    • post infectiou sencephalitis

    • cerebellar ataxia

    • nystagmus

    • tremor

  • neonatal varicella

    • 1wk before birth to 4 weeks after

    • give VZIG within 92hrs

exclusion

  • exclusion from kindergarten/school

    • exclude for 1wk from date of appearance of rash
