History on confirmed UTI

  • poor urine flow

  • history suggesting previous uti or confirmed previous UTI

  • recurrent fever uncertain origin

  • antenatally -diagnosed renal abnormal

  • family history of VUR or renal disease

  • constipation

  • dysfunctional voiding

  • enlarged bladder

  • abdominal mass

  • evidence of spinal lesion

  • poor growth

  • high blood pressure

Pyelonephritis

if bacturia and fever >38 then acute peylonephritis

CRP shouldn’t be used to distinguish upper and lower uti

routine imaging in localisation of UTI not recomended

Definitions

  • febrile UTI/peylonephritis

    • infection of upper kidney and lower urinary tract with fever

  • cystitis

    • infection of lower urinary tract wihtout fever

  • atypical

    • uti associated with sepsis or bactraemia

    • concern regarding obstructive uropathy

    • failure to respond to abx within 48hrs

    • associated impaired renal function

    • infection with non E.Coli

  • BPAC definition: atypical

    • >38deg

    • poor or minimal urine flow

    • septicaemia

      • fever

      • floppy increase HR/RR

    • palpable abdominal or bladder mass

    • if initially treated as typcial:

      • failure to respond to treatment within 48hrs

        • strongly suggests pyeloneprhitis

Treatmnet

  • admission:

    • \<3mo

    • atypical

  • consider \<6mo

    • discuss with consultant

  • oral abx if >5mo who are not toxic/septic

    • 7d if febrile

    • 3d if not

  • Emperic antiobiotics

    • specific urinary symptoms

    • children >3mo to \<3yr with non specific symptoms

      • (fever, lethargy, abdominal pain)

    • children >3yo with urine dipstick positive for nitrites

    • children >3yo with urine dip + leuc and urinary symptoms

  • E.coli = 75%

  • Co-trimoxazole (trimethoprim + sulphamethoxazole) 4+20mg/kg (0.5mL/kg) bd for 3d

  • cefaclor 10mg/kg bd for 3d

  • cephalexin 12.5mg/kg bd for 3d

  • Amox clavulanate 10mg/kg tds for 3d

  • short courses = long courses

  • drink plenty and don’t hold on

    • sufficeient fluids in small amounts

  • review of treatment in 48hrs

Capturing urine
