Sudden infant death in infancy

Sudden infant death in infancy
  • Safe sleep
  • Cause

  • declining

  • death of an infant \<1yo

    • initially unexplained

      • SIDS if remains unexplained

    • usually asleep

  • 0.8/1000 live births - 50/year

  • NZ one of highest rates of SUDI

  • Maori infants = overwhelming majority

    • 5x higher

  • high risk behaviour

    • maternal smoking

    • poorer knowledge about SUDI risk factors

Safe sleep

  • pace infant to sleep on back with face-up

  • ensure infant’s face clear of bedding

    • cannot become trapped or starngled

    • pillows and bumper pads avoided

    • not soft surfaces/loose bedding

    • no gaps in beds

  • infant to sleep in own cot, bassinet, wahakura or pepi-pod

    • in same room

  • infants should not sleep with another person

  • provide smoker-free environment

    • pregnancy

    • after birth

  • breast feed

Cause

  • infant in critical developmental period

    • 1mo to 1yr

    • 90% \<5mo

    • peak 1-4mo

  • underlying vulnerability present since birth

    • low birth weight

    • abnormal in arousal system

    • serotonin receptor abnormal

    • genetic polymorphisms

      • calcium -> cardiac dysrhythmia

  • exposure to an external stressor

    • asphyxiation

    • re-breathing of expired gases

    • overheating

  • modifiable risk factors

    • sleep position

      • supine compared to prone

      • prone increase 3-14x SUDI

      • side sleeping alo increase risk

    • bed sharing

      • risk highest in infants \<3mo

      • risk increase if infant is sharing bed with:

        • preson who smoker

        • consumed etoh

        • taken drugs

    • matresses, mattress protectors/bumpers

      • if not desinged for infants

        • increase risk of SUDI

      • new / good condition

    • overheating

      • comfortable

      • no more than 1 extra layer than an adult would wear to be comfortable

      • 18-22deg

    • swaddling

      • firmly wrapping young infants in light sheeting or muslin

      • conflicting evidence

      • increase sleep time adn decrease arousal

        • may increase risk of SUDI

    • cigarette smoke

    • breast feeding

      • protectiveeffect

      • exclusively until age 6mo

      • continued breast feeding with complemetary until 1yr

    • immunisation

      • decrease risk fo SUDI

        • misconception DTaP at increase rirsk but no association

    • Dummy/pacifier use

      • reduced risk

      • not well understood

      • but:

        • malocclusion

        • increase risk of dental caries

        • otitis media

        • earlier cessation of breaast feeding

      • if dummy used not before breast feeding firmly established

        • 3-4wk
