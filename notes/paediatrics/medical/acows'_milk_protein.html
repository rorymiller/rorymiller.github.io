soy formula and elemental formula = 2 main products used for infants with CMPA

immunologically mediated adverse reaction of cows’ milk protein

approx 2% \<2yo

type can be differentiated by timining of reaction in relation to intake of cows’ milk

immediate

  • most serious form = anaphylaxis

  • in inafants features of aanaphylaxis not always apparent

    • coughing

    • wheezing

    • severe distress

    • floppiness

    • collapse

  • extremely rare in breast fed babies

  • often allergic to other types of foods

    • eggs peanuts

  • 80% resolve by age 3yrs

Food protein-induced enterocolitis syndrome

  • relatively uncommon non-IgE mediated reaction

  • rapid onset of:

    • projectile vomiting

    • floppiness

    • pallor

    • poss. diarrhoea

  • 1-3hrs after ingestion of cows’ milk

  • ddx:

    • gastroenteritis

    • sepsis

    • intestinal obstruciton

  • usually occurs when cows’ milk first introduced

Atopic eczema

  • allergy should be considered possible cause (that is not responding to appropriate treatment)

  • pruritic rash = severe

  • most (not all) igE mediated

Gastrointestinal syndrome

  • GORD

    • 40% of infants referred for management of GORD

      • underlying cause CMPA

    • typical symtposm:

      • frequent regurgitation

      • poor feeding

      • aversion to feeding

    • not usually IgE mediated

    • may inmprove partially with PPI

    • usually resolve by age 12 and 18mo

  • Allergic eosinophilic gastroenteritis

    • weight loss and Failure to thrive

      • associated with:

        • vomiting

        • diarrhoea

        • severe irritability

        • blood loss

      • iron def. and protein losing enteropahty can occur in severe cases

  • food induced enteropathy

    • persistent diarrhoea

    • perianal excoriation

    • failure to thrive

    • vomiting

    • anaemia/hypoproteinamia

    • chronic malabsorptoin

      • intestinal villous damage

    • associated with soy allergy or secondary lactose intolerance common

  • constipation and infantile colic

    • controversial

    • unequivocal diagnosis

    • both bommon and causes multi-factorial

    • elimination of cows’ milk may reoslve symptom

    • colic usually resolves 4-6mo

    • consipation 12-18mo

  • food induced proctocolitis

    • mild diarrhoea low grade rectal bleeding

      • mucous and fleck sof blood

    • first 3mo of life

      • most develop tolerance to CMPA by 12mo

  • eosinophilic oesophagitis

    • more common in older children than infants

      • usual features refusal of food

      • failure to thirve

      • vomiting

      • reflux

      • poor response to anti-reflux

    • require endoscopy

    • based on histological finding of eosinophilia of oesophagus

Diagnosis

  • challenging

  • referral

    • urgent

      • anaphylaxis

      • severe failure to thrive

      • hypoproteinaemia/protein losing eneropathy

        • vomiting

        • diarrhoea

        • severe irritability

        • failure to thrive

        • iron deficiency anaemia

      • food protein inudced proctocolitis

        • low grade rectal bleeding

    • if elmination has failed

      • haematemesis

      • chronic diarrhoea

      • persistent vomiting

      • persistent rectla bleeidng

      • persistent iron deficiency anaemia

      • severe eczema

Managmenet

  • coews’ milk should be removed and replaced with elemental/soy based

  • continue breast feeding whenever

    • should be eliminated from maternal diet where anaphylaxis

    • if delayed reaction

      • maternal intake often tolerated

      • avoidance usually only necessary if residual symptoms

    • if IgE mediated

      • avoidance stricly enforced with provision of allergy action plan/adrenaline autoinjector

  • changed for soy

    • not appropriate \<6mo

      • cross-reactivity or concurrent soy allergy much higher in this group

      • 25% under 6mo vs. 5% 6-12mo

    • some infnats >6mo find soy formular more tolerable than extensively hydrolysied

    • not subsidised

    • comparably priced to standard cows’ milk formula
