PID

PID
  • Management
  • Follow-up

  • usually caused by STI

  • testing:

    • vulvovaginal swab

    • high vaginal

      • BV

      • trichomoniasis

      • cadidiasis

    • +/- anorectal

    • bimanual exmianiton

      • pelvic masses or tenderness

    • urine pregnancy test and urinalysis dipstick

    • serology for HIV and syphilis

    • FBC, CRP

    • vital sounds

  • clinical diagnosis

    • history

    • clinical findings

      • pelvic pain AND

      • uterine tenderness or adnexal tenderness or cervical motion tenderness

    • Additional supportive features

      • abnormal cervical or vaginal mucopurulent discharge

      • fever >38

      • elevated WBC/CRP

      • confirmed infection with an sti or BV

    • results of tests

Management

  • First line:

    • ceftriaxone 500mg im

      • + 2mL lignocaine 1%

    • plus doxycycline 100mg bd for 14d

    • plus metronidazole 400mg bd for 14d

      • metronidazole can be discontinued in women with mild PID symptoms if not tolerated

    • consider azithromycin 1g stat with repeat dose in 7d instead of doxy

      • espeically if poor compliance

    • abstain from sex 14d and/or until 7d after all sexual contacts treated

  • notification

    • 2mo

    • sexual health check

      • if asymptomatic treat emperically for chlamydia with azithromycin 1g po stati

  • IUD users

    • PID treatment not hindered bu presence of IUD

    • left in situ should be made case-case

    • if remove

      • apprx. 24hrs and consider ECP if unprotected sex in previous 7d

        • if inadequate response

          • IUD removal considerd

Follow-up

  • followup 72hors moderate / severe

    • repeat bimanual exam

      • no unprotected sex?

      • tolerated medication?

      • notifiable contacts informed?

      • any risk of reinfection?

  • follow-up 1-2 weeks mild PID

    • as above

    • offer repeat
