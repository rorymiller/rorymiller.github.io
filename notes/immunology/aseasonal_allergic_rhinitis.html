can have a significant impact on peoples’ lives

often co-exists with asthma

Rhinitis

  • Seasonal allergic rhinitis

    • associated early spring and early summer

    • outdoor allergens

  • pereniial allergic rhinitis

    • all year round

    • house dust mite

    • pets

    • mould

  • occupational rhinitis

    • worsened at work

    • triggered by chemicals

    • irritants

    • dust

  • non allergic rhiniitis

    • strong smells

    • change temperture

    • viral infecitons

    • pregnancy

    • hypothyroidism

    • antihypertensives

  • family history known risk factor

  • symptoms:

    • sneezing

    • congestion

    • watery anterior rhinorrheoa

    • itchy nose, eyes and thorat

    • sinus pressure

    • facial pain

    • decrease smell or taste

    • children:

      • tiredness

      • daytime sleepiness

      • sniffing

      • blinking

      • eye rubbing

      • speech problems

      • snoring / dark circles

  • not usually associated

    • unilateral

    • nasal obstruction

    • mucopurlent rhinorrhoea

    • post. rhinorrhoea with thick mucous

    • recurrennt epistaxis

  • towards end of pollen season symptoms may worsen

    • allergen priming

Ask about:

  • pattern, chronicity and seasonality of symptoms

  • response to medications

  • occupational exposure

  • environmental history

  • identification of ppt. factors

  • effect on qol

  • co-existing asthma

in reality people have both:

calssified by:

  1. duration of symptoms

    1. intermittent - \<4d/wk or 4wk at time

    2. persistent - >4d/wk or 4wk at time

  2. severity

    1. mild - no troublesome symptoms with normal sleep/daily activiries

    2. moderate - severe

      1. troublesome symptoms with abnormal sleep and impairment of daily activiries

management

mild

  • intranasal antihistamines

    • equal to or more effective than oral antihistamine

    • less effective than intranasal corticosteroids

    • not as effective symptoms related to eye and thorat

    • rapid onset

    • progress to intranasal corticosteoid

    • azelastine hydrochloride 140mcg/acutation 1 spray each nostril

      • not subs

  • oral antihistamines

    • if spray not acceptable

    • less effective for nasal congestion

    • eye symptoms better compared to intranasal

    • second generation

    • none superior than tohers

    • certirizine may cuase drowsiness

  • sedating antihistmaine CI in children

    • somnolence cont. thorugh next day and affect cognitive function

Moderatie = severe

  • intranasal corticosteroid

    • 4 main symptoms

      • sneezing

      • itching

      • rhinorrhoea

      • nasal blockage

      • onset within 12hrs

      • maximum efficacy2 weeks

    • advice re sprays

      • shake bottle

      • look down at floor

      • use right hand for left

        • aim to side away frrom septum

      • don’t sniff

    • Beclomethasone 50-100mcg/nostril twice daily

    • from age 5yo budesonide 50mcg once daily PS

  • ssaline spray/drops

    • less effective

    • can relieve nasal congestion/dryness

    • 1/4 tsp salt with 2 cups of cooled, boiled water

  • intranasal decongestants

    • risk of rhinitis medicamentosa (rebound nasal congestion)

      • used short term \<10d and intermitetntly

  • oral decongestants

    • pseudoephedrine and phenylephrine

      • not recommended for use in hary fever

      • associated with

        • insomnia

        • hypertension

        • palpitations

  • oral corticosteroids

    • very severe or intractable nasal symptoms or nasal polyps

      • cont. intranasal during treatmnet

  • parenteral corticosteroids

    • not recommended

  • intranasal anticholinerics

    • add-on

    • no effect on other nasal symptoms

    • reduce rhinorrhoea

  • intranasal sodium cromoglycate

    • effective preventing onset of symptoms

    • less effective than intranasal corticosteroids
