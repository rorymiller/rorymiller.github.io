Skin prick tests

  • preferred test

  • not requested if no oging to prove management of patinet

  • detects IgE bound to mast cells in skin

  • “wheal and flare” reaction which is maximal after about 15-20mins

  • extent of reaction assessed + or - result

  • if ≥ 3mm more than negative control = positive result

    • children \<5 much smaller wheal size can be clinically significant

  • wheal size:

    • >15mm = very sensitive

    • 10-15mm moderately sensitive

    • 5-10mm mildly sensitive

    • \<3mm negative result

  • if swelling ,heat, itch 1-2hrs after = late phase cutaneous reaciton

    • don’t appear to relate to clinical situation

    • no diagnositc value

  • negative control = saline solution

    • if react to neg control then extermetly sensitiv eand results unreliable

    • dermographism

  • positive control = histamine

    • everyone react

    • failure to react = taking antihistamines

  • patient selection

    • may not be reliable for older adults

    • skin reactivity increase with age until 50-60yo

    • darker pigmented skin

      • develop larger wheals than people with paler skin

    • \<2yo then @ allergy clinic as more difficult to interpret results

      • small wheal but large erythematous flare

    • pregnant women

      • only if detection of allergy and subsequent treatment outweighs risk

        • uterine contractions may occur as a result of systemic reaction

  • indications

    • significant asthma, eczema, allergic rhinitis

      • avoidance or desensitisation may improve condition

    • when major allergen avoidance measures are being considered

      • removal of carpets

      • family pet

      • avoidance of specific foods from diet

    • acute urticaria and angioedema

      • related to food allergy

    • significant systemic symptoms following bee or wasp sting

    • usually not helpful in management of chronic urticaria

      • rarely manifestation off allergy

  • contraindication

    • patient wiht recent history of anaphylaxis

      • not be performed within 4-6wks

      • results falsely negative

    • cuation in people with significant co-morbidities

      • cardiovascular disease

      • arrhythmia

      • also elderly people

    • patinets with skin conditions such as dermographism

      • widespread atopic dermatitis

    • medicines that interfere

      • antihistamine H1blockers

        • h2 work primarlily in stomachd

      • topical corticosteroids

        • 2-3wks prior

        • oral / inhaled don’t interfere

      • TCA

      • phenothiazine,

      • BDZ

      • quetiapine

      • mirtazepine

  • choosing appropriate allergens

    • small number of allergens - food

    • more allergens (up to 25) in rhinitis, eczema or asthma

  • interpreting

    • wheal size resultgs can be affected

      • false positive and flase negatives

        • psitive reaction from one test site may impact another

        • 2cm apart

    • sensitivity doesn’t = allergy

    • negative skin prick to foods such as peanut =

      • NPV >95%

      • PPV signifiantly lower

    • lack of sensitivity doesn’t maen that allergy can be ruled out

    • wheal size cannot predicty severity of an allergic reaction

  • advantages

    • cost - $24/panel compared to 40-50$ per allergen

    • greater sensitivity and NPV compared to serium allergen secific igE

      • relatively specific

    • results available quickly

  • disadvantages

    • can trigger anaphylaxis

    • risk higher in younger children

    • not all allergens availbale

    • takes 30min or more to perfomr at community lab

serum allergen specific igE tests

  • formally known as RAST

  • useful when skin prick testing unsuitable

    • dermographism

    • unable to discontinue antihistamine

    • rural areas - skin prick testing unavailable

    • when suspected allergen not available

    • history strongly suggest allergy to speicifc allergen but skin prick testing negative

    • specific safety concerns due to past anaphylaxis

    • predicution of allergy persisitence or resolution of food allergies in children

  • less sensitive than skin prick tests

  • false positives are less common with serum testing

    • PPV greater

  • selecting allergens

    • shouldn’t be used as general screening tests

stinign insect (venom) allergy

  • most produce transient local reaction

  • last up to several days

  • resolves without treatment

  • marked local swelling extending from sting site = igE late phase reaciton

  • life threatening reactions 0.4-0.8% children and 3% adults

  • 5-10% = risk fo systemic reaction who experience large local reaction

  • patients who have hsitory of a systemic reaction to an insect sting should:

    • carry emergency adrenaline for self-administration

    • carry medical identification for stinging insect hypersensitivity

  • indications for testing:

    • systemic allergic reaction

      • ensure appropriate immunotherapy administered

      • testing not indicated local reactions
